About Us

RunNUS is an annual running event organized by NUS Students’ Sports Club to promote healthy lifestyles among the NUS population and the public.

Since its inception in 2007, RunNUS has become one of the biggest and most anticipated event for NUS students, staff and alumni. Above running and fun, RunNUS will be partnering with NUS Disability Support Office (DSO) and inviting Social Service Organizations (SSOs) this year.



The National University of Singapore Students’ Sports Club (NUSSSC) is one of the most active and widely reached student-run organization on campus. Dedicated to providing the NUS population with an array of sporting activities and a channel to enrich their campus lives, we cater to the recreational sporting needs of the NUS community. Ever since its inception in 1979, NUSSSC has grown into the largest non-faculty constituent club in NUS, currently led by its 38th Management Committee; our objective is to promote a healthy lifestyle and community bonding through recreational sports. NUSSSC boasts a family of 21 member clubs, categorised into Martial Arts, Indoor, Land and Water sports. In line with our vision of encouraging the NUS community in their pursuits of sports, NUSSSC also spearheads major events such as NUS Biathlon, SunNUS, NUS Sports Camp, RunNUS, Martial Arts Day (MADNUS) and Inter-Faculty Games (IFG).

The club also hosts the following 6 projects:

NUS Biathlon 24 March
SunNUS 9 June
Sports Camp 2-5 July
Martial Arts Day (MadNUS) 21 August
Inter-Faculty Games August - September
RunNUS 9 September

Apart from our usual 10km and 5km running categories, RunNUS is back with a new walking category, 3km Challenge, open to everyone! To promote inclusiveness, we will be inviting 50 Persons With Disabilities / Special Needs (PWDSNs) to join us in our walk.

3KM

  • Be grouped with our PWDSNs and interact with them throughout the 3km journey.
    1. Only 50 slots available so hurry!
    2. Completely free of charge
    3. Feeling unsure/worried about not being experienced enough? Fret not as:
      1. You will go through a talk conducted by Disabled... Disabled People’s Association (DPA) on how to interact with our PWDSNs. More details will be given at a later date.
      2. Trained personnel will be provided by SSOs on the event day to aid in the smooth execution of the 3km challenge.
    4. Important: Before signing up, please ensure that you will be able to commit your time on the day of the talk and the event as any withdrawal will not be permitted.
  • Pair up and walk with your family/friend
    1. Sign up individually and pair up with a family member/friend on the event day.
    2. Each pair will be given a blindfold on the event day.
    3. Person A will be blindfolded while Person B guides Person A.
    4. Note: Person A and B can switch at any point in time, as the pair deems suitable. Blindfolding is NOT compulsory but strongly recommended for participants to challenge themselves.
